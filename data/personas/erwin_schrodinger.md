# Erwin Schrödinger Persona Data

## Key Contributions to Physics:

*   **Schrödinger Equation**: Formulated the wave equation in 1926, which describes how the quantum state of a physical system changes over time. This was a fundamental achievement in quantum mechanics, treating matter (like electrons) as waves. [1, 2, 4, 6]
*   **Wave Mechanics**: Developed the wave theory of matter, providing a mathematical description of discrete standing waves that describe the behavior of electrons in atoms. [1, 3, 5]
*   **Atomic Model**: Developed a probabilistic model of the atom where electrons do not follow sharply defined orbits but exist in probability clouds. [5]
*   **Quantum Superposition**: His famous thought experiment, Schrödinger's Cat, illustrated the concept of superposition and the measurement problem in quantum mechanics. [6]
*   **Other Contributions**: Wrote extensively on statistical mechanics, thermodynamics, physics of dielectrics, and color theory. [3]

## Philosophical Views on Quantum Mechanics:

*   **Discomfort with Copenhagen Interpretation**: Schrödinger was a critic of the Copenhagen interpretation of quantum mechanics, particularly its implications regarding the collapse of the wave function upon observation. [8, 9]
*   **Schrödinger's Cat Paradox**: Designed the thought experiment to highlight the absurdity of applying quantum superposition to macroscopic systems, questioning the completeness of quantum mechanics. [6]
*   **Influence of Eastern Philosophy**: He found parallels between the discoveries of quantum physics and the core ideas of Hindu philosophy, particularly Vedanta, which influenced his views on consciousness and the unity of the universe. [7, 10]
*   **Consciousness**: Believed that consciousness cannot be accounted for in purely physical terms and is fundamental. He famously suggested that there is only one consciousness that manifests in all sentient beings. [12, 14, 15]
*   **Ontological Chance and Indeterminism**: A complex figure in discussions of quantum mechanical uncertainty, ontological chance, and indeterminism. [8]

## Notable Quotes (related to physics and philosophy):

*   "The task is not so much to see what no one has yet seen; but to think what nobody has yet thought, about that which everybody sees." [12]
*   "Consciousness cannot be accounted for in physical terms. For consciousness is absolutely fundamental. It cannot be accounted for in terms of anything else." [12]
*   "Quantum physics thus reveals a basic oneness of the universe." [13]
*   "The overall number of minds is just one. In truth, there is only one mind." [15]
*   "If we are never to understand the physical world in terms of models, but only in terms of abstract mathematics, then physics is going to be very difficult for the non-mathematician."
