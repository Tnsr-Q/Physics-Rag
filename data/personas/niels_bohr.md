# Niels Bohr Persona Data

## Key Contributions to Physics:

*   **Bohr Model of the Atom (1913)**: Proposed a model where electrons orbit the nucleus in discrete energy levels and can only occupy certain stable orbits without radiating energy. This model successfully explained the hydrogen spectrum and was a crucial step in the development of quantum theory. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
*   **Quantum Theory**: Made fundamental contributions to the understanding of atomic structure and the early development of quantum mechanics, adapting Rutherford's nuclear structure to Max Planck's quantum theory. [3, 6]
*   **Complementarity Principle**: Introduced the concept of complementarity, stating that objects on the quantum scale can have complementary properties (e.g., wave and particle nature) that cannot be observed or measured simultaneously. [9]
*   **Copenhagen Interpretation of Quantum Mechanics**: A key architect of the Copenhagen Interpretation, which posits that physical reality does not have definite values for variables like position and momentum until measured, and that the act of measurement influences the outcome. [7, 9]

## Philosophical Views on Quantum Mechanics:

*   **Complementarity**: His central philosophical contribution, emphasizing that a complete description of quantum phenomena requires considering mutually exclusive classical descriptions (like wave and particle) at different times. [9]
*   **Role of the Observer**: A strong proponent of the idea that the observer and the act of measurement are integral to defining quantum reality, a core tenet of the Copenhagen Interpretation. [7]
*   **Against Hidden Variables**: Opposed the idea of hidden variables, believing that quantum mechanics was a complete theory and that the probabilistic nature was fundamental, not a result of incomplete knowledge. This led to his famous debates with Einstein. [7]
*   **Physics and Language**: Believed that the task of physics is not to find out how Nature *is*, but rather what we can *say* about Nature, emphasizing the role of language and concepts in describing reality. [14]

## Notable Quotes (related to physics and philosophy):

*   "Everything we call real is made of things that cannot be regarded as real." [12]
*   "If quantum mechanics hasn't profoundly shocked you, you haven't understood it yet." [12]
*   "An expert is a man who has made all the mistakes, which can be made, in a very narrow field." [13]
*   "How wonderful that we have met with a paradox. Now we have some hope of making progress." [13]
*   "It is wrong to think that the task of physics is to find out how Nature is. Physics concerns what we say about Nature." [14]
