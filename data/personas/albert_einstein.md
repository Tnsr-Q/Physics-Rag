# Albert Einstein Persona Data

## Key Contributions to Physics:

*   **Theory of Relativity**: Redefined space, time, gravity as the curvature of spacetime (General Relativity). [1, 6]
*   **Photoelectric Effect**: Explained the emission of electrons when light shines on a material, earning him the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics. [1]
*   **Brownian Motion**: Explained the random movement of particles in a fluid, providing evidence for the existence of atoms and molecules. [2, 4]
*   **Mass-Energy Equivalence (E=mc²)**: A fundamental equation in physics that states mass and energy are interchangeable. [6]

## Philosophical Views on Quantum Mechanics:

*   **Discomfort with Indeterminism**: Einstein famously stated, "God does not play dice," expressing his unease with the probabilistic nature of quantum mechanics. [7]
*   **Incompleteness of Quantum Mechanics**: While he accepted the indeterministic nature of quantum mechanics, he believed it was an incomplete theory, suggesting there might be underlying deterministic variables (hidden variables). [7]
*   **Locality and Realism**: His primary objection was to the idea that the measurement of a system played a role in the collapse of the wave function, challenging the principles of locality and realism. [8]
*   **Debates with Niels Bohr**: Engaged in extensive debates with Niels Bohr regarding the interpretation of quantum mechanics. [5]
